Crater Lake's West Rim · Camp at Diamond Lake

Optional: Crater Lake Coffee & Food Park (Eagle Point, on Hwy 62) · open-air food park, easy RV parking
11:30
Rim Visitor Center & Sinnott Memorial Observation Station~1h
Rim Visitor Center & Sinnott Memorial Observation Station: I'd strongly suggest making this your first stop after entering the park. The Sinnott overlook is built right on the cliff edge — not only are the lake views superb, there's also a huge 3D relief model of the terrain. For elementary- and middle-school-age kids it's a wonderfully vivid, hands-on intro to volcanic geology. Kids can also pick up a Junior Ranger activity booklet at the visitor center.
12:45
Watchman Overlook — picnic~1h
Watchman Overlook: Driving along the open west-rim road, this is the closest and most jaw-dropping viewpoint for the iconic island in the lake (Wizard Island). Since you all love camping and the outdoors, pack some food up from down the hill beforehand and find an open spot near the overlook for a family picnic — sipping the alpine breeze while taking in that deep-blue water is pure bliss.
14:00
Discovery Point Trail — hike~1h
Discovery Point Trail: Another classic west-rim trail. The terrain is relatively gentle and it's usually one of the first stretches in the park to melt out. It's very easy — perfect for a relaxed hike with two kids — winding between evergreen forest and the open caldera rim, with the lake revealed from ever-changing angles the whole way.
16:00
Arrive camp · Diamond Lake Loop C lakeside

Before You Go

Confirm and book these before departure.

  • Confirm travel dates — sets whether you can summit Lava Butte and how open Crater Lake is
  • Check Crater Lake conditions — see the conditions page at nps.gov/crla before leaving
  • Newberry caldera fee — $5/vehicle, or bring a Northwest Forest Pass
  • Lava Butte summit (if towing a small car, before mid-June) — grab a free timed permit at the welcome station; vehicles over 22 ft can't go up
  • Book campsites early — Caspar Beach, River Park RV (Grants Pass), Diamond Lake, Lake Siskiyou; Crane Prairie is first-come dispersed (no booking)
  • Gear — fire steel, plastic gold pan, kayaks + PFDs, warm layer, grippy shoes
Drive safe. Catch crabs. Climb glass. NorCal ↔ Oregon · 6-Day RV Loop · ~1,250 miles · times are RV-paced estimates, leave 30–60 min of slack
