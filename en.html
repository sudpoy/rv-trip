NorCal ↔ Oregon
6-Day RV Loop Wild Coast, Crater Lakes & Volcanic Glass

From Sunnyvale, head up the wildest stretch of coast, cross crater lakes and Central Oregon's volcanic country, then roll home down I-5.

Three Key Route Notes

  • ▸Day 2: Leave Fort Bragg via Hwy 20 through Willits to US-101 — don't take Hwy 1 to Leggett (off-limits to big RVs). Start at 6:30 AM.
  • ▸Day 3: Enter and exit Crater Lake from the south (Hwy 62), then take Hwy 97 north — the Rim Drive loop and north entrance are usually still closed in early June.
  • ▸Day 4: Lava Butte's summit shuttle isn't running yet in early June and rigs over 22 ft can't drive up, so the "summit" payoff comes from the obsidian flow inside the caldera.

Before You Go

Confirm and book these before departure.

  • Confirm travel dates — sets whether you can summit Lava Butte and how open Crater Lake is
  • Check Crater Lake conditions — see the conditions page at nps.gov/crla before leaving
  • Day 2 tide table — time the Sue-meg agate hunt around low tide
  • Newberry caldera fee — $5/vehicle, or bring a Northwest Forest Pass
  • Lava Butte summit (if towing a small car, before mid-June) — grab a free timed permit at the welcome station; vehicles over 22 ft can't go up
  • Book campsites early — especially Crown Villa/Sun Outdoors Bend and Caspar Beach
  • Gear — fire steel, plastic gold pan, kayaks + PFDs, warm layer, grippy shoes
